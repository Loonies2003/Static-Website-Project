Tips for exercises and diet

1) Set realistic goals:

Achievable targets can help prevent discouragement and build momentum. Begin with manageable workout goals like short, 8-minute sessions and gradually increase as you get comfortable.

2) Track your progress:

Keep a record of your workout achievements in a journal or a fitness app to monitor your workout frequency and improvements. This can be a strong motivational tool for your morning exercise.

3) Go to bed earlier:

Getting enough sleep helps you feel rested and ready for morning workouts. Adjust your evening routine to sleep earlier, and start a relaxing pre-bed routine like reading or meditation instead of using electronic devices.

4) Start with enjoyable workouts:

You're more likely to stick with a routine that you enjoy, so choose exercises that you find fun and engaging. Experiment with different types of workouts to find what excites you.

5) Be patient and persistent:

Understand that habit formation takes time. Patience and consistency are needed for long-term success. Stay committed, and don't get discouraged by occasional setbacks.
